# Route: RESEARCH NARRATIVE

**Fires when** the subject is how something developed over time: a project's architecture, a field's ideas, a decision's history, an incident's unfolding.

**Primary surface:** timeline or annotated visual essay.

**Read alongside:** techniques/relationship-grammar.md (threads and junctions are relationships), techniques/visual-thesis.md, techniques/evidence.md, techniques/semantic-scaling.md for long spans.

## Mandatory mechanics

- **Threads, not just points.** A subject persists across the timeline; its thread thickens, thins, forks, or ends. A row of dated events is a log, not a narrative.
- **Junctions are interactive and evidence-linked.** Where two threads converge or one forks, the junction opens onto the actual source: the message, commit, document, or decision where it happened.
- **Evidence evolution is shown, not just endpoints.** What was believed at each stage, and what changed the belief.
- Time scrubbing where the span is long: dimming everything after a chosen moment shows the state of knowledge as it was then.
- Threads that died are shown dying, not omitted. Abandoned directions are content.

## Considerations

- Distinguish what happened from what it meant; the interpretation layer is separate from the chronology.
- Retrospective coherence is a trap: the story looked less inevitable at the time, and the deliverable should preserve the contingency.
- Where the record is incomplete, the gap is drawn, not smoothed over.

## Conclusion form

Trajectory; inflection points; where this is heading and on what evidence.

## Intermediates delta

plan.md: the thread inventory (what persists across time) and the junction list. dossier.md: each junction entry carries the source artifact it opens onto.

## Anti-patterns

- A dated bullet list styled as a timeline.
- Junctions that are decorative dots with no underlying evidence.
- A clean arc that hides the abandoned branches.
