# Example Sound_Design.md: Broadside

A completed aesthetic direction document for a 1895-1930 naval combat sim, showing the specificity each section needs. Use as the model when authoring; do not copy across projects.

## 1. Listener perspective
Tactical god-cam hovering at low altitude near the engaged fleet: sounds are heard across open water from a raised vantage, close enough that a firing turret is physical, far enough that distant fire arrives late and filtered. Shipyard Mode is the exception: a quiet drafting office, 2D, intimate.

## 2. Emotional targets
- Combat: weighty, concussive, dread-awe. Anti-targets: arcade-snappy, movie-trailer boom, sci-fi whoosh.
- Ambience: patient, isolating, mechanically alive. Anti-target: busy/cluttered.
- Shipyard/UI: tactile, drafting-room, deliberate. Anti-target: digital bleeps.

## 3. Mix hierarchy
- T1 (owns the moment; owns the low end): main battery fire, torpedo/magazine detonations.
- T2 (low-mids): secondary guns, armor impacts, shell splashes.
- T3 (mids/highs, short): alarms, engine-order telegraph, turret traverse, shell flight.
- T4 (recessed bed): engine, deck, sea, and fire ambiences.
No T3/T4 clip may be described with dominant or huge; nothing but T1 claims deep low end.

## 4. Palette cohesion rules
- Exteriors: open-water air, long rolling tails that decay over water; no room reverb.
- Interiors (turret, engine room): tight steel reflections, short decay.
- Everything: coal-and-steel grit; never clean, synthetic, or musically reverbed.
- Loops: mechanical rhythm audible but irregular enough to avoid metronome feel.

## 5. Dynamics plan
Loudest moment: a full broadside. Quietest: the ranging silence between salvo and splash; this gap is a designed beat, do not fill it. Ambience beds sit low enough that a single secondary gun reads as an event.

## 6. Reference anchors
- Emulate: Greyhound (distance-delayed heavy gunfire; the dread of waiting for fall of shot), Master and Commander (deck-level concussion and rigging-free mechanical detail, shifted forward a century), Sea Power (tactical-distance layering of a whole engagement).
- Avoid: World of Warships (arcade punch, compressed sameness), generic trailer impacts (sub-drops, braams).
