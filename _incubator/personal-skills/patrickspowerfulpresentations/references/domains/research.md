# Route: RESEARCH

**Fires when** the reader's task is to understand a landscape, field, or body of evidence, rather than to choose among options or understand one system's mechanism.

**Primary surface:** landscape / taxonomy. Comparison supports it; a matrix is a supporting page, not the lead.

**Read alongside:** techniques/evidence.md (mandatory), techniques/visual-thesis.md, techniques/charts.md if the field has quantitative structure.

## Page archetypes

| Page | Question it answers |
|---|---|
| Landscape | what exists, and how does it cluster |
| Taxonomy / structure | on what axes does the field actually divide |
| Detailed comparison | how do the members of one cluster differ |
| Maturity or trajectory | what changed recently, what is stable, what is contested |
| Disagreements | where do credible sources conflict, and on what |
| Sources & methodology | mandatory on this route |

## Considerations

- Clusters are argued, not asserted. A taxonomy page states the axis it divides on and why that axis rather than another.
- Conflicting evidence is shown, not flattened. Where the field disagrees, both positions appear with their support.
- Callouts explain structure: why a cluster exists, what a recent change did to the field, where a comparison across clusters is misleading.
- Recency matters more here than on most routes; state the as-of date prominently, because a landscape is a snapshot.
- Distinguish what is documented from what is inferred about the field itself, not only about individual items.

## Conclusion form

Key structural findings; unresolved uncertainties; implications; **what evidence would change the conclusion**. A forced default-pick recommendation is wrong on this route; if the reader wanted a pick, the route is comparison or buying.

## Intermediates delta

plan.md adds: the division axis and its justification. dossier.md entries include the source's own position where the field is contested, not only the value.

## Anti-patterns

- A matrix of everything, across clusters that do not share axes.
- A confident synthesis that hides the disagreement it papered over.
- Undated claims about a moving field.
